Field Scouting Guide

1 Pick 5+ spots across the field — avoid end rows, waterways, and obviously good or bad areas. You want an honest average.

2 Measure your row length for 1/1000th acre (use the calculator above — it adjusts for your row spacing). A tape measure or rangefinder works, or pace it off at roughly 3 ft per stride.

3 For corn: Count every harvestable ear in that length. Then pull 5+ representative ears and count kernel rows around the cob and kernels per row from butt to tip. Skip the tiny unfilled tip kernels.

4 For soybeans: Count every plant in that length. Then pull 10+ representative plants and count total pods per plant. Shake out a few pods to verify seeds per pod (usually 2–3).

5 Average your samples and enter the numbers above. Do this in multiple fields — conditions can vary widely even on the same farm.

These are estimates. Actual yield can differ 10–15% or more due to kernel weight variation, harvest loss, moisture, tip-back, ear droppage, and stalk quality. Use these numbers for planning and marketing decisions, not as a guarantee.
