Micronutrient Reference — WI / MN Crop Production

Deficiency thresholds, responsive crops, and typical application rates. Soil and tissue testing should confirm deficiency before applying — most WI/MN fields on non-sandy soils are sufficient.

NutrientMost Responsive CropsDeficient (soil)Soil Test MethodTypical RateKey Notes
Sulfur (S) Soybeans, alfalfa, small grains, canola <10 ppm KCl extraction 10–20 lb S/ac Greatest risk: sandy soils, low OM, fields leached by high rainfall. AMS (24% S) is common source. Symptoms: yellowing of new growth.
Zinc (Zn) Corn, beans, soybeans <0.5 ppm DTPA DTPA extraction 1–2 lb Zn/ac soil
0.5 lb Zn/ac foliar		 Risk: high pH soils (>7.0), high P, cold wet spring. Striped leaves on corn ("white bud"). Soil apply every 4–5 years or foliar as needed.
Boron (B) Alfalfa, clover, sunflower, canola <0.5 ppm Hot water extraction 0.5–1 lb B/ac Risk: sandy soils, low OM, drought. Corn and soybeans rarely respond in WI. Alfalfa shows "hollow stem." Do not over-apply — toxic range is narrow.
Manganese (Mn) Soybeans, oats, wheat <5 ppm DTPA DTPA extraction 0.5–1 lb Mn/ac foliar Risk: high pH soils (>6.5), organic soils, poorly drained. Soybean IDM (iron-deficiency-like). Foliar is preferred — soil Mn availability is pH-dependent.
Iron (Fe) — IDC Soybeans (Iron Deficiency Chlorosis) pH >7.3 + CaCO₃ Field observation / SPAD Foliar or IDC-tolerant varieties Primary fix: plant IDC-tolerant/resistant varieties. Interseeding oats reduces severity. Foliar iron: multiple applications needed. Most common on calcareous soils in MN.
Chloride (Cl) Wheat, corn <15 ppm Soil extraction 15–30 lb Cl/ac Reduces take-all root rot in small grains. KCl (MOP) provides Cl. Avoid foliar application — leaf scorch. More relevant in western MN/ND wheat production.

Economic Thresholds — Corn & Soybean Insects

Thresholds are population levels at which the cost of control equals the value of crop loss prevented. Treat at or slightly before threshold — not after. Scouting frequency and accuracy are critical.

Herbicide Mode of Action — WSSA Groups

Rotating herbicide groups is the single most effective tool for managing weed resistance. Never use the same group twice in one season. Target waterhemp, Palmer amaranth, and kochia with at least two effective groups.

Resistance alert — WI/MN: Waterhemp has confirmed resistance to Groups 2, 5, 9, 14, and 27 in the Upper Midwest. Giant ragweed resistant to Groups 2 and 9. Marestail (horseweed) resistant to Group 9. Always use a residual herbicide with a different MOA — do not rely solely on post applications.
GroupMOA / Site of ActionKey Products (examples)Common Weeds ControlledResistance Risk
1 (A) ACCase inhibitors (fatty acid synthesis — grasses) Quizalofop (Assure II), sethoxydim (Poast), clethodim (Select Max), fluazifop Annual grasses: foxtail, barnyard grass, crabgrass. No broadleaf control. High
2 (B) ALS inhibitors (acetolactate synthase) Imazethapyr (Pursuit), Classic (chlorimuron), Pursuit Plus, Canopy, FirstRate, Harmony Broad spectrum. Many grass and broadleaf weeds. Waterhemp, giant ragweed widely resistant. Very High
3 (K1) Microtubule assembly inhibitors (dinitroanilines) Trifluralin (Treflan), pendimethalin (Prowl), ethalfluralin Pre-emergence annual grasses and small-seeded broadleaves. Incorporated or surface applied. Low
4 (O) Synthetic auxins (auxin mimics) 2,4-D, dicamba (XtendiMax, Engenia, FeXapan), clopyralid (Stinger), MCPA Broadleaf weeds. Corn-safe pre-plant/pre-emerge. Dicamba requires certified applicator in Xtend systems. Low
5 (C1) PSII inhibitors — triazines Atrazine, metribuzin (Sencor), simazine, cyanazine Broad spectrum broadleaf and grass. Waterhemp resistant in many fields. Corn rotations only (atrazine carryover). Moderate
6 (C2) PSII inhibitors — ureas/amides Bentazon (Basagran), bromoxynil Post broadleaf control in soybeans and corn. Bentazon: velvetleaf, cocklebur, soybean safe. Low
9 (G) EPSP synthase inhibitors (shikimate pathway) Glyphosate (Roundup, Durango, many generics) Broad spectrum. Do not use alone post-emergent in Roundup Ready systems — always tank mix with a different group. Very High
10 (H) Glutamine synthetase inhibitors Glufosinate (Liberty, Interline) LibertyLink system. Broad spectrum contact. Most effective on small weeds. Requires good coverage. Low
14 (E) PPO inhibitors (protoporphyrinogen oxidase) Lactofen (Cobra), fomesafen (Flexstar), flumioxazin (Valor), saflufenacil (Sharpen), acifluorfen Broadleaf weeds incl. waterhemp, pigweed, ragweed. Excellent residual PRE option. Waterhemp resistance growing — do not use alone repeatedly. Moderate
15 (K3) Very long-chain fatty acid inhibitors (chloroacetamides) Acetochlor (Harness, Degree), S-metolachlor (Dual Magnum), dimethenamid (Outlook) PRE annual grasses and small-seeded broadleaves. Excellent residual. Essential component of any resistance management program. Low
27 (F2) HPPD inhibitors (4-hydroxyphenylpyruvate dioxygenase) Mesotrione (Callisto), tembotrione (Laudis), topramezone (Impact), isoxaflutole (Balance) Corn-selective. Bleaching of new growth. Good waterhemp control. Resistance developing — tank mix required. Moderate
