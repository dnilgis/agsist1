How to Use This Grain Bin Calculator

Enter your bin diameter and eave height (or length, width, and depth for rectangular storage), choose your crop, and the calculator instantly shows total bushel capacity, weight, and — if you enter a market price — estimated net value after moisture shrink and drying costs. The depth slider lets you model a partially filled bin. Use the Hopper option for bins with an inverted-cone floor; the calculator automatically adds the hopper cone volume to your total.

Understanding Moisture Shrink

Grain delivered above standard moisture loses weight — and value — during drying. The industry standard shrink formula is (Moisture In − Standard Moisture) × 1.3. The 1.3 multiplier covers both the water removed and a small handling loss allowance. Corn at 18% moisture heading into a 15.5%-standard market shrinks 3.25%, costing roughly 85 to 100 bushels on a 3,000-bushel truckload. Common Questions

How many bushels in a 30-foot bin?
A 30×24 ft round bin holds about 11,317 bu level-fill at standard moisture. Add a peaked cone surface for ~12,700 bu.
What is pack factor?
Grain settles 0.5–1.5% over time as kernels compact. Leave it at 0 for a conservative estimate. Use 1% for a bin that’ll sit all winter.
Hopper bottom vs. flat floor?
A hopper bin holds slightly less than a flat-floor bin of the same wall height because the cone takes space a flat floor would fill solidly.
How do I figure drying cost?
Multiply points to dry × $/pt/bu. Drying from 18% to 15.5% = 2.5 pts. At $0.035/pt: 2.5 × $0.035 = $0.0875/bu.
